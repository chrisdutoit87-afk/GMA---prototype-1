📊 Key Research Findings

GMs spend 1–3 hours preparing each session

Combat encounter design — choosing enemies, calculating balance, drawing maps — is the single highest time-cost task. Critically, it is one of the few parts of session prep that cannot be improvised at the table. Everything else (NPC dialogue, plot hooks, descriptions) can be adjusted on the fly. Combat encounters cannot. Source: SlyFlourish DM Survey; community forum analysis.

PF2e's XP budget system is the most mathematically precise encounter design framework in any major TTRPG

Pathfinder 2e uses a creature-level-relative XP budget system with five defined threat levels (Trivial/Low/Moderate/Severe/Extreme). Expert analysts consistently rate it as more accurate and GM-friendly than D&D's CR/XP system. GMA implements this as a deterministic algorithm, never an AI estimate. Source: SlyFlourish comparative analysis; RPGBOT technical critique; GM Core Remastered.

Lore accuracy is the primary differentiator — and no current tool provides it

An NLP study (ACL Proceedings 2025) found that even well-prompted large language models produce incorrect D&D/PF lore answers for a significant percentage of monster queries. GMA addresses this by grounding generation in a structured knowledge base sourced from the Archives of Nethys and official Pathfinder bestiaries. Source: ACL 2025 — "Lore Coherent Encounter Generation"; DungeonSolvers KFC review.

No existing tool combines balance + lore + map in one workflow

The market is divided into three isolated categories: encounter calculators (Kobold+, Prep n' Pray), map tools (Inkarnate, Dungeondraft), and full VTTs (Roll20, Foundry). GMs currently use 3–5 tools to achieve what GMA delivers in one session. The gap is not theoretical — it is an active, voiced community-level unmet need. Source: GMA Insight Matrix; 55-source research bank.

🗺 Roadmap
Planned improvements  |  Post-MVP Development

The current prototype validates the core concept: lore-accurate enemy generation, deterministic PF2e difficulty calculation, and a linked tactical map. These are the confirmed next steps, sequenced by research priority and community demand.

Phase 2A Legal
D&D 2024 Content Strategy & Legal Review
Determine the precise boundary between SRD-safe and proprietary WotC content before any D&D monster database is populated. Establish the reference-and-link approach. D&D 2024 support is the highest-traffic opportunity — but it requires legal sign-off first.
Phase 2B Core Engine
Validated PF2e Rules Engine — Deterministic Difficulty
Build and validate the creature-level XP budget calculator for PF2e, cross-checking against 20+ known encounter examples from Archives of Nethys. Zero discrepancies between GMA difficulty output and manual calculation is the acceptance criterion.
Phase 2C Content
Structured PF2e Lore Knowledge Base
Build the verified creature habitat, faction, and ecological constraint database from Archives of Nethys and the Monster Core. Ten test encounter prompts should produce zero ecologically impossible combinations before this phase is considered complete.
Phase 2D UI / UX
Real GM User Testing (5–10 GMs, All Experience Tiers)
Show the prototype to new GMs, experienced GMs, and system-switchers. Key questions: can a new GM complete the input form without asking a question? Do experienced GMs trust the difficulty label before verifying it?
Phase 2E Feature
Advanced Map Generation — Environment-Aware Terrain
Upgrade map output from grid-and-colour to properly procedurally generated terrain: dungeon walls, forest cover, elevation indicators, hazard placement, and environmental features derived from the scene description. Aesthetic polish is post-phase.
Phase 2F System
D&D 2024 (5.5e) Support — Conditional on Legal Review
Implement the three-tier XP budget system (Low/Moderate/High) from the 2024 DMG, validated against SlyFlourish's Lazy Encounter Benchmark and RPGBOT's difficulty analysis. This is the highest-volume user acquisition opportunity in the current market.
Phase 3 Business
Freemium Monetisation — Free Tier + GM Tier
5 free encounter generations per month with full feature access. GM Tier (~$5–7/month) unlocking unlimited generations, save history, PDF export, and priority generation. Campaign Tier (~$10–12/month) adding homebrew lore injection and multi-encounter organisation.
Phase 3 Feature
Lock & Swap Controls — Granular Encounter Editing
Allow GMs to lock individual enemies and regenerate the rest, swap a single enemy type while keeping the others, and manually override difficulty tier post-generation. This is the feature that turns the tool from a random generator into a GM creative assistant.
Phase 4 Expansion
Additional System Support — Starfinder 2e & Beyond
Pathfinder's sibling system Starfinder 2e uses a compatible XP budget framework, making it the lowest-effort expansion target. Additional systems (OSR games, other Paizo products) to follow based on community demand data from feedback submissions.
Phase 4 Content
Premium Encounter Pack Marketplace
A curated marketplace where experienced GMs can publish and sell setting-specific encounter packs (e.g. "Whispering Tyrant's Undead Legions", "Absalom Street Encounters"). One-time purchase model, similar to DMs Guild. Secondary revenue stream alongside subscriptions.
